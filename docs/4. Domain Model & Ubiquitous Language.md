# Chapter 7
# Domain Model & Ubiquitous Language
## Establishing the Vocabulary of the Product

**Version:** 0.1

**Priority:** Critical

**Purpose**

This chapter establishes the official vocabulary used throughout the Household Meal Decision Assistant.

Every future document should use these definitions consistently.

Whenever ambiguity exists, this document takes precedence.

---

# 1. Why This Chapter Exists

One of the biggest reasons software projects become difficult to maintain is that different people use the same word to mean different things.

Consider this simple conversation.

Product Manager:

> Recommend meals.

Engineer:

> I recommended recipes.

Designer:

> I designed dish cards.

Data Scientist:

> I ranked ingredients.

All four people believe they are discussing the same feature.

They are not.

The purpose of this document is to eliminate ambiguity.

---

# 2. Core Philosophy

Our product models **household food decisions**.

Not recipes.

Not cooking.

Not groceries.

Not nutrition.

Therefore our vocabulary should reflect decisions rather than culinary terminology.

# 28. Naming Principles

Whenever introducing a new concept,

ask three questions.

Is it observable?

Is it measurable?

Does it influence recommendations?

If the answer is "no" to all three,

it probably should not become a first-class entity.

# 29. Meal Slot

Definition

A Meal Slot is a specific eating occasion on a specific date.

Example: "Tuesday, July 8th — Dinner."

A Meal Slot may be

Filled — a meal was cooked and consumed.

Skipped — the household ate out, ordered delivery, or skipped.

Planned — a recommendation has been accepted but not yet cooked.

Empty — no decision made yet.

Meal Slots differ from Meals.

A Meal Slot is the container.

A Meal is the content.

The recommendation engine operates on empty Meal Slots.

The learning engine learns from filled and skipped Meal Slots.

---

# 30. Cooking Event

Definition

One continuous period of cooking that produces one or more dishes.

A Cooking Event differs from an Eating Event.

Example

A mother cooks lunch at 11 AM.

The family eats at 1 PM.

The son eats the same food at 6 PM.

This is one Cooking Event and two Eating Events.

Attributes

Start Time

End Time

Cook

Dishes Produced

Leftovers Generated

Effort Level

Tracking Cooking Events separately from Eating Events allows the system to learn effort patterns, cooking durations, and batch cooking behaviour.

---

# 31. Decision Context

Definition

The complete set of signals available at the time a recommendation is generated.

A Decision Context is a first-class entity because it allows the learning engine to correlate *why* a recommendation was made with *how it was received*.

Attributes

Date and Time

Meal Slot

Inventory Snapshot

Active Restrictions

Available Time

Household Members Present

Weather

Day of Week

Season

Upcoming Events

Recent Meal History

Household Twin State

Every recommendation should be permanently linked to its Decision Context.

This enables the learning loop to answer:

"Given this exact context, was this recommendation accepted or rejected?"

A classification grouping for ingredients.

The recommendation engine must reason about ingredient categories to ensure variety, nutrition balance, and substitution.

Top-Level Categories

Root Vegetables (Potato, Carrot, Beetroot)

Gourds (Bottle Gourd, Bitter Gourd, Ridge Gourd)

Cruciferous (Cauliflower, Cabbage, Broccoli)

Legumes (Rajma, Chana, Moong, Toor)

Dairy (Milk, Curd, Paneer, Ghee)

Grains (Rice, Wheat, Jowar, Bajra)

Proteins (Eggs, Chicken, Fish, Paneer, Tofu)

Oils and Fats

Spices and Aromatics

Fruits

Categories support

Variety tracking ("too many root vegetables this week")

Nutritional balance ("not enough leafy greens")

Substitution ("no cauliflower? try broccoli")

Shopping recommendations ("your legume stock is low")

# 33. Dietary Profile

Definition

A composite entity representing the complete dietary picture of a household member or the household as a whole.

Examples

Vegetarian

Jain (no onion, no garlic, no root vegetables)

Eggetarian

Vegan

Pescatarian

Lactose Intolerant

Diabetic Friendly

Gluten Free

A Dietary Profile aggregates multiple Restrictions and Preferences into a coherent pattern.

Unlike individual Restrictions which are simple rules, a Dietary Profile has complex interactions.

Example: Jain = Vegetarian + No Onion + No Garlic + No Root Vegetables + No Mushroom.

This cannot be modeled as independent restrictions without a grouping concept.

Dietary Profiles may be

Permanent (Vegetarian household member)

Periodic (Fasting on specific days)

Temporary (Post-surgery dietary restriction)

# 34. Leftover Transformation

Definition

A mapping from one or more leftover items to potential new dishes.

This models the creative reuse behaviour described throughout the product knowledge base.

Structure

```
Source Leftover  →  Transformation  →  New Dish

Cooked Rice      →  Stir-fry       →  Fried Rice
Cooked Rice      →  Temper          →  Lemon Rice
Cooked Rice      →  Shape + Fry     →  Rice Cutlets
Chapati          →  Roll + Fill     →  Chapati Roll
Dal              →  Knead into dough →  Dal Paratha
Paneer Curry     →  Stuff into bread →  Paneer Paratha
```

Attributes

Source Leftover

Maximum Age (food safety window)

Required Additional Ingredients

Effort Level

Resulting Dish

The recommendation engine should proactively suggest transformations before leftovers expire.

# 35. Household Event

Definition

A planned or recurring occasion that temporarily changes household meal behaviour.

Types

Festival (Diwali, Holi, Eid, Christmas, Navratri)

Life Event (Birthday, Anniversary)

Guest Visit (expected guests)

Travel (household members away)

Academic (exam periods, school vacations)

Health (illness, post-surgery recovery)

Attributes

Type

Date or Date Range

Recurrence Pattern (annual, monthly, weekly)

Impact on Restrictions (e.g., fasting during Navratri)

Associated Food Traditions (sweets during Diwali)

Affected Members

Household Events differ from daily restrictions because they modify the entire decision context for a bounded period.

# 36. Feedback Signal

Definition

A structured record of how a household responded to a recommendation.

Types

Accepted — the recommendation was cooked.

Modified — the recommendation was partially followed (e.g., cooked the main dish but changed sides).

Rejected — the recommendation was explicitly dismissed.

Ignored — the recommendation was neither accepted nor dismissed.

Saved — bookmarked for future consideration.

Liked — explicit positive signal (thumbs up).

Disliked — explicit negative signal (thumbs down).

Attributes

Recommendation ID

Decision Context ID

Signal Type

Reason (optional, free text or structured)

Timestamp

Every Feedback Signal should update the Household Twin.

Feedback Signals are the primary fuel for the learning loop.

---

# 37. Notification Rule

Definition

A rule governing when and why the system initiates contact with the household.

Types

Preparation Reminder — "Soak chana tonight for tomorrow's chole."

Leftover Alert — "Yesterday's dal should be used today."

Restocking Reminder — "Rice stock appears low."

Tomorrow Planning — "Would you like to plan tomorrow's meals?"

Festival Preparation — "Diwali is in 3 days. Plan special meals?"

Expiry Warning — "Spinach should be used today."

Attributes

Trigger Condition

Timing

Frequency Cap (avoid notification fatigue)

Priority

Delivery Channel (push, voice, in-app)

Principle

Every notification should be actionable.

No notification should require more than one tap to address.

Notifications should decrease as the Household Twin improves, not increase.

# 38. Regional Cuisine Profile

Definition

A structured model describing how a regional cuisine tradition organises meals.

Examples

### North Indian

Typical Meal Structure: Roti/Paratha + Sabzi + Dal + Rice + Salad + Pickle

Primary Cooking Fats: Ghee, Mustard Oil

Staple Grains: Wheat, Rice

### South Indian

Typical Meal Structure: Rice + Sambar + Rasam + Poriyal + Curd + Pickle + Papad

Primary Cooking Fats: Coconut Oil, Sesame Oil

Staple Grains: Rice

### Gujarati

Typical Meal Structure: Roti + Sabzi + Dal + Rice + Kadhi + Farsan + Sweet

Primary Cooking Fats: Groundnut Oil, Ghee

Staple Grains: Wheat, Rice, Bajra

Attributes

Meal Component Template

Default Ingredient Taxonomy

Common Spice Combinations

Typical Cooking Methods

Festival-Specific Foods

Regional Cuisine Profiles allow the recommendation engine to assemble *meals* rather than merely suggesting isolated *dishes*.

When the system recommends "Dal Fry," the Regional Cuisine Profile determines whether to pair it with "Jeera Rice and Roti" (North Indian) or "Steamed Rice and Papad" (South Indian).

# 39. Household Twin — Architectural Clarification

The Household Twin has been described in multiple chapters.

To eliminate ambiguity, this section clarifies its nature.

The Household Twin is a **hybrid model**.

It contains both

**Structured attributes** (explicit, queryable)

- Member preferences (numeric scores)
- Active restrictions
- Dietary profiles
- Household event calendar
- Inventory state

**Emergent patterns** (learned, probabilistic)

- Household rhythm
- Effort preferences by day
- Repetition tolerance
- Seasonal behaviour shifts
- Acceptance prediction
- Cooking style classification

The structured layer is a database.

The emergent layer is a model.

Together they form the Household Twin.

Neither alone is sufficient.

# Closing Thoughts

A surprising amount of product complexity disappears once the language becomes precise.

By clearly distinguishing between a **meal**, a **meal slot**, a **dish**, a **recipe**, an **ingredient**, an **ingredient category**, a **leftover**, a **leftover transformation**, a **cooking event**, a **decision context**, a **feedback signal**, and a **recommendation**, we create a shared vocabulary that will influence every future discussion, every database schema, every API, every AI model, and every UX decision.

This chapter has been expanded from its original version to include ten additional entities identified during a critical review of the product knowledge base. These entities were consistently discussed across multiple chapters but had never been formally defined. Their inclusion ensures that the domain model is comprehensive enough to support the recommendation engine, the Household Twin, and the learning loop as described throughout the product documentation.
