# Business Model & Engagement
## Sustaining the Household Companion

**Version:** 1.0

**Status:** Draft

**Priority:** Medium

**Consolidates:** Chapters 13 & 14

---

# 1. Guiding Constraint

> The business model must never compromise the product's ability to reduce decision fatigue. Gamification must never increase cognitive load.

---

# Part I: Monetization

## 2. Business Model Principles

**Free Core.** Reducing meal decision fatigue should be free. If a household must pay before experiencing relief, adoption fails.

**Monetize Enhancement, Not Necessity.** Revenue from features that enhance, not gate essential functionality.

**Households Over Advertisers.** Advertising that influences recommendations is strictly prohibited.

**Value Alignment.** Users should feel that paying improves *their* experience.

---

## 3. Monetization Options

### Option A: Freemium Subscription

**Free Tier:** Core recommendation engine, basic Household Twin, meal history, basic inventory, up to 4 members.

**Premium Tier:** Advanced Twin with rhythm detection, voice interaction, photo-based inventory, weekly planning, leftover transformations, festival planning, unlimited members, cross-device sync, household analytics.

Estimated: ₹99-199/month or ₹999-1499/year (India). $4.99-9.99/month (international).

### Option B: Grocery Marketplace Integration

Recommendation-aware shopping list becomes a purchase flow via grocery delivery partners. Revenue: affiliate commission.

**Guardrail:** Shopping list serves the household first. Never promote products for commission over household needs.

### Option C: B2B Licensing

License Household Twin and recommendation engine to smart appliance manufacturers, grocery retailers (anonymized demand planning), health platforms (anonymized dietary trends).

**Guardrail:** No B2B arrangement compromises individual household privacy. All B2B data anonymized beyond re-identification.

---

## 4. Recommended Phasing

| Phase | Focus | Monetization |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| Launch → 100K HH | Product-market fit, retention, Twin accuracy | Free for all |
| 100K → 500K HH | Conversion rate, willingness to pay | Introduce premium tier |
| 500K+ HH | Ecosystem | Grocery integration, B2B licensing |

---

## 5. Anti-Monetization

Never: Promoted recipes (pay-to-rank). Ingredient brand sponsorships. Selling individual household data. Advertising within recommendations. Pay-to-remove-ads.

---

## 6. Unit Economics (Illustrative)

Cloud AI inference: ~$0.01-0.05/recommendation. On-device: effectively free. Cloud sync: ~$0.10-0.50/household/month.

At 5% free-to-premium conversion:
- 100K households → ₹50-75 lakh/year.
- 500K households → ₹2.5-3.75 crore/year.
- Grocery affiliate revenue is additive.

# Part II: Gamification

## 7. The Risk

Most gamification rewards *engagement* — app opens, profile completion, social sharing. Our product's mission is the opposite. We want users to spend *less* time thinking about food.

If gamification makes users spend more time in the app without reducing decision fatigue, it has failed.

---

## 8. JTBD Alignment

Every gamification element must connect to a specific job:

| Element | Job Connection |
|---------|---------------|
| Reduced waste streak | Reduce food waste |
| Variety achievement | Increase variety without forcing experimentation |
| Home cooking streak | Encourage home cooking over delivery |
| Balanced nutrition week | Improve nutritional balance |
| Leftover hero | Use leftovers creatively |
| New dish explorer | Expand household repertoire |

Elements without a job connection should not exist.

---

## 9. Outcome-Based Rewards

### What to Reward
- "This week, you used 94% of your vegetables before they expired."
- "You cooked with 12 different vegetables this week, up from 8."
- "20 consecutive home-cooked days."
- "This week included leafy greens 3×, legumes 4×, dairy 5×."
- "You transformed leftovers into 3 new dishes."

### What NOT to Reward
App opens. Profile completion. Recipe views. Time in app. Social sharing. Notification clicks.

---

## 10. Household Milestones

- **The First Month:** "30 meal decisions together."
- **The Hundred Meals:** "Your Household Twin understands you."
- **Zero Waste Week:** "No vegetables expired."
- **Festival Traditions:** "Diwali with traditional meals for the second year."
- **Diversity Champion:** "5 regional cuisines this month."

---

## 11. Persona-Specific Gamification

| Archetype | Resonant Rewards |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Traditional | Festival streaks, seasonal cuisine, family recipe count |
| Dual-Income | Time saved, meal prep achievements, weekday simplicity |
| Young Children | "Kids approved" count, hidden vegetable success, lunchbox variety |
| Budget-Conscious | Cost per meal, budget adherence, grocery efficiency |

---

## 12. Anti-Patterns

**Never create pressure.** Not "You broke your streak!" but "15 of the last 20 days home-cooked. Impressive."

**Never compare households.** All gamification is inward-facing and self-referential.

**Never gate features.** Achievements are celebrations, not gates.

**Never interrupt the core journey.** Gentle, dismissible summaries only.

---

## 13. Implementation Priority

**Phase 1 (Post-MVP):** Weekly summary, basic outcome tracking.

**Phase 2:** Monthly milestones, household achievements, diversity tracking.

**Phase 3:** Seasonal challenges, family traditions, long-term evolution visualisation.

**Gamification should never appear in the MVP.** Core recommendation experience must prove its value first.
